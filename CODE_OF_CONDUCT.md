# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and constructive.
- Focus criticism on ideas and code, not people.
- Accept responsibility, apologize when appropriate, and learn from mistakes.
- Protect other people's credentials, private conversations, and personal data.
- Respect the project's read-only, local-only, and legal-compliance boundaries.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, insults, or sexualized attention.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Posting access tokens, cookies, private conversations, or unredacted logs.
- Encouraging credential theft, account abuse, or bypassing service safeguards.
- Disrupting project spaces or repeatedly ignoring maintainer guidance.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, security
reports, release channels, and public spaces where someone is representing the
project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Do not report conduct incidents in a public Issue when the report contains
private or identifying information. Use the repository's
**Security → Report a vulnerability** form and begin the report title with
`Conduct report`.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may warn, temporarily ban, or
permanently ban participants whose behavior is inappropriate, threatening, or
harmful. Reports will be reviewed confidentially and fairly, with disclosure
limited to what is necessary to investigate and respond.
